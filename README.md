# Bangkok Airbnb Data Analysis 
In this project, I built a Power BI dashboard to analyze Airbnb listings in Bangkok. My goal was to understand how guests experience the city, how prices vary across neighbourhoods, and which areas concentrate the highest activity. By combining KPIs, visual insights and geospatial analysis, I created a clear and accessible overview of the market that helps identify patterns in guest preferences, pricing behaviour and neighbourhood performance.

On the first page, I wanted to give a quick but complete snapshot of the Airbnb market in Bangkok. I focused on the most important KPIs, what guests value the most, and how listings are distributed across the city. This page sets the foundation for understanding the scale of the market and the key factors that shape guest experiences.

**Conclusions**
- I found that Bangkok has a very active Airbnb market, with a high number of listings and reviews.
- Guests consistently value check‑in and communication the most, which highlights the importance of host interaction and smooth arrival experiences.
- Vadhana stands out as the neighbourhood with the highest concentration of listings and a slightly higher average price, suggesting strong demand in that area.
- The mini ranking confirms that the most central and popular neighbourhoods dominate the supply, which aligns with the distribution shown on the map.

On the second page, I focused on understanding how prices and ratings behave across different neighbourhoods. I wanted to explore whether higher prices lead to better ratings, and which areas perform best in terms of guest satisfaction.

<img width="1863" height="1050" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0b44deab-ad35-4b25-a108-2432f73299eb" />

**Conclusions** 
- The overall average rating is very high, which tells me that most guests have positive experiences in Bangkok.
- Nong Khaem appears as the highest‑rated neighbourhood, while Thawi Watthana has the lowest ratings, showing clear differences in perceived quality across the city.
- When looking at the price distribution, I noticed that prices do vary between neighbourhoods, but the differences are not extremely large. Most areas fall within a similar price range, which suggests a relatively consistent pricing structure across Bangkok.
- The scatter plot shows that there is no clear correlation between price and rating. Higher prices do not necessarily lead to better ratings, and many affordable listings still achieve excellent scores.
- This indicates that guest satisfaction is influenced more by service quality, location, and host interaction than by price alone.
